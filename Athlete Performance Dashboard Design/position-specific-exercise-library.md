# Position-Specific Exercise Library
## Detailed Drill Progressions for Quarterback, Point Guard, and Center Back

---

## QUARTERBACK (FOOTBALL) - COMPLETE EXERCISE LIBRARY

### TECHNICAL SKILLS PROGRESSION

#### 1. FOOTWORK & MECHANICS

**Level 1 - Beginner (Weeks 1-4)**

*Drill 1.1: Stance and Grip Foundation*
- Duration: 5 minutes
- Reps: 20 repetitions
- Description: Establish under-center and shotgun stance, proper ball grip with laces
- Coaching Cues:
  - Feet shoulder-width, knees slightly bent
  - Ball held at chest level, two hands
  - Laces positioned for quick release
  - Eyes up, scanning imaginary defense
- Progression: Add snap simulation, increase speed

*Drill 1.2: Three-Step Drop*
- Duration: 8 minutes
- Reps: 15 throws
- Description: Quick rhythm drop for short routes (slants, quick outs)
- Coaching Cues:
  - First step: Open hip to throwing side
  - Second step: Gather, weight on back foot
  - Third step: Plant and throw
  - Timing: Ball out by step 3
- Common Errors: Drifting backward instead of gaining depth, late release
- Progression: Add defensive read, vary routes

*Drill 1.3: Five-Step Drop*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Intermediate depth drop for medium routes (curls, comebacks)
- Coaching Cues:
  - Rhythm: Quick, quick, quick, quick, plant
  - Depth: 7 yards from line
  - Balance on plant foot
  - Hips closed toward target on throw
- Progression: Seven-step drop, play-action variations

**Level 2 - Intermediate (Weeks 5-8)**

*Drill 1.4: Movement in Pocket*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 15 scrambles
- Description: Slide, climb, and escape collapsing pocket while maintaining eyes downfield
- Coaching Cues:
  - Maintain throwing stance (don't lean)
  - Two hands on ball until decision made
  - Climb up through rush, avoid lateral drift
  - Reset feet before throw
- Equipment: Cones or agility bags to simulate rushers
- Progression: Add live rushers, extend play scenarios

*Drill 1.5: Play-Action Mechanics*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 12 throws
- Description: Fake handoff, deepen drop, throw to intermediate routes
- Coaching Cues:
  - Sell the fake (extend ball fully to RB)
  - Quick pullback and hide ball
  - Deepen drop 3 yards beyond normal
  - Locate target during drop
- Progression: Bootleg action, naked bootleg with sprint-out

**Level 3 - Advanced (Weeks 9-12)**

*Drill 1.6: Extended Play Creation*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 10 scrambles
- Description: Break contain, create off-schedule, improvise completions
- Coaching Cues:
  - Keep play alive (don't force)
  - Communicate with receivers (thumbs up for scramble drill)
  - Find throwing lanes
  - Slide when appropriate
- Progression: Defender pursuit, full team chaos drill

*Drill 1.7: Quick Game Mastery*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Pre-snap reads, fast release vs. blitz, hot routes
- Coaching Cues:
  - Identify blitzer pre-snap
  - Communicate protection/hot route
  - Release under 2.0 seconds
  - Anticipatory throw (before WR turns)
- Progression: Disguised blitzes, zone blitzes with delayed rushers

#### 2. THROWING PROGRESSION

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 2.1: Form Throwing (No Drop)*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 30 throws
- Description: Stationary throwing focusing on release mechanics
- Coaching Cues:
  - Elbow above shoulder on release
  - Wrist snap with laces rotation
  - Follow-through to opposite hip
  - Weight transfer front foot
- Distance: 10-15 yards
- Progression: Increase distance, add movement

*Drill 2.2: Route Tree Introduction*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 5 throws per route (9 routes = 45 total)
- Routes: Hitch, slant, out, in, comeback, post, corner, go, crossing
- Description: Learn trajectory and timing for each route concept
- Coaching Cues:
  - Match ball speed to route (fast for out, touch for corner)
  - Lead receiver appropriately
  - Vary trajectory (laser vs. arc)
- Progression: Combine routes in progressions

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 2.3: Progression Reads (2-3 Options)*
- Duration: 25 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Read primary, secondary, check-down in sequence
- Coaching Cues:
  - Pre-snap ID primary based on coverage
  - Post-snap confirm and throw or move to #2
  - Don't lock on, let eyes flow naturally
  - Commit to throw when open
- Progressions examples:
  - Smash concept: Corner/curl read
  - Levels: Deep/intermediate/flat
  - Stick: Stick/swing read vs. coverage
- Progression: Add 4th and 5th options, disguised coverage

*Drill 2.4: Touch and Velocity Control*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 30 throws (15 touch, 15 velocity)
- Description: Alternate between touch passes (arcing) and fastballs
- Coaching Cues:
  - Touch: Higher release, more arc, softer landing
  - Velocity: Low release, line drive, tight spiral
  - Situation dictates (sideline = touch, slant = velocity)
- Targets: Cones at 20-30 yards for touch, partner at 15 yards for velocity
- Progression: Game situation callouts (coach yells route type)

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 2.5: Full-Field Progressions (4-5 Options)*
- Duration: 30 minutes
- Reps: 25 throws
- Description: Complete NFL-style progressions with coverage variations
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read coverage shell (single-high, two-high, quarters)
  - Primary determined by leverage/numbers
  - Move through progression based on coverage rotation
  - Check-down vs. pressure
- Example progressions:
  - Verticals: Read safeties, throw to open seam
  - Flood: Read flat defender, throw corner/seam/flat
  - Four verts: Read middle safety, alert to motion side
- Progression: Disguised coverages, post-snap rotation, pressure

*Drill 2.6: Anticipation Throws*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 15 throws
- Description: Throw before WR makes break, trust timing and route
- Coaching Cues:
  - Release as WR plants to break
  - Trust route depth and timing
  - Ball arrives as WR turns
  - Essential vs. man coverage
- Routes: Slant, out, comeback, dig
- Progression: Contested reps with DB, increase distance

*Drill 2.7: Back-Shoulder Mastery*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Place ball away from DB, only WR can catch
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read DB leverage (inside = back-shoulder)
  - Underthrow slightly, ball arrives high and behind
  - Communication with WR pre-snap
  - Requires touch and arc
- Progression: Live DBs, various routes (fade, comeback, post)

#### 3. DECISION-MAKING & READS

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 3.1: Coverage Recognition Classroom*
- Duration: 15 minutes (non-field)
- Method: Film study with coach
- Content: Identify Cover 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 pre-snap
- Keys:
  - Safety depth and alignment
  - Corner leverage
  - Linebacker depth
- Progression: Add disguises, post-snap recognition

*Drill 3.2: Single-Read Drills*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 15 throws
- Description: Pre-determined primary target, throw if open or throw away
- Coaching Cues:
  - Know answer pre-snap (this is primary)
  - Post-snap confirm open/closed
  - If closed, throw away or scramble
  - Don't force
- Progression: Add check-down option (2 reads)

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 3.3: Two-Option Progressions*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Concepts: Hi-lo, inside-out, levels
- Description: Primary/secondary read based on defender
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read specific defender (usually LB or safety)
  - If defender takes A, throw B
  - Quick decision (<1.5 seconds)
- Progression: Three options, faster tempo

*Drill 3.4: Blitz Recognition & Hot Routes*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 15 throws
- Description: Identify overload blitz, communicate hot route, deliver quickly
- Coaching Cues:
  - Count rushers pre-snap
  - Alert protection and hot route
  - Quick release (<2.0 seconds)
  - Ball out before pressure arrives
- Progression: Disguised blitzes, zone blitzes

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 3.5: Post-Snap Rotation Diagnostics*
- Duration: 25 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Recognize coverage rotation after snap, adjust read accordingly
- Coaching Cues:
  - Pre-snap ID likely coverage
  - Post-snap watch safety movement
  - Adjust progression if rotation occurs
  - Example: Cover 2 rolls to Cover 3, change primary
- Progression: Multiple disguises, rapid-fire reps

*Drill 3.6: Pressure Decision Matrix*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 15 scrambles
- Description: Under live or simulated pressure, make optimal decision
- Decision tree:
  - Hot route open? Throw immediately
  - Can climb pocket? Move and reset
  - Scramble lane? Run for yards
  - Nothing? Throw away
- Coaching Cues:
  - Don't panic, process quickly
  - Protect the football
  - Live to play next down
- Progression: Increase pressure intensity, add coverage disguises

#### 4. VELOCITY & ARM STRENGTH

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 4.1: Long Toss Progression*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Distance: Start 20 yards, build to 50+ yards
- Description: Gradual distance increase to build arm strength safely
- Coaching Cues:
  - Arc ball, don't overthrow flat
  - Full arm extension on release
  - Use legs to generate power
  - Stop if arm fatigues
- Frequency: 2-3x per week maximum
- Progression: Increase max distance by 5 yards per week

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 4.2: Velocity Throws with Radar*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 max-intent throws
- Description: Measure ball velocity, train max-effort mechanics
- Coaching Cues:
  - Full effort but controlled
  - Explosive hip rotation
  - Snap wrist through release
  - Record velocities to track progress
- Target velocity: 50-55 mph intermediate, 55-60 mph advanced
- Progression: Increase avg velocity over 8-12 weeks

*Drill 4.3: Comeback/Deep Out Emphasis*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 20 throws
- Description: Routes requiring zip and velocity
- Coaching Cues:
  - Low release point, line-drive trajectory
  - Ball must beat DB to spot
  - 18-20 yard depth on comebacks
  - Precision more important than max velocity
- Progression: Increase distance, add DB pressure

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 4.4: Tight-Window Fastballs*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 15 throws
- Description: Thread ball through narrow windows at high speed
- Setup: Two cones 3 feet apart at 15-20 yards
- Coaching Cues:
  - Maximum velocity with accuracy
  - Visualize throwing through keyhole
  - Compact release
- Progression: Narrow window, increase distance, add movement

## POINT GUARD (BASKETBALL) - COMPLETE EXERCISE LIBRARY

### TECHNICAL SKILLS PROGRESSION

#### 1. BALL-HANDLING

**Level 1 - Beginner (Weeks 1-4)**

*Drill 1.1: Stationary Two-Ball Foundation*
- Duration: 8 minutes
- Reps: 4 sets of 30 seconds each variation
- Variations:
  - Simultaneous bounce (both balls together)
  - Alternating bounce (right-left rhythm)
  - High-low (one high dribble, one low)
  - Figure-8 through legs
- Coaching Cues:
  - Head up, not watching balls
  - Fingertip control, not palms
  - Low athletic stance
  - Same height on both dribbles
- Progression: Add movement, increase speed

*Drill 1.2: Basic Moves Mastery*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 10 reps each move, each hand
- Moves: Crossover, between legs, behind back, in-and-out
- Coaching Cues:
  - Sharp changes, not looping
  - Sell with shoulders and eyes
  - Low and quick
  - Protect ball with off-hand
- Setup: Stationary, then add cone to simulate defender
- Progression: Combine moves, add speed

*Drill 1.3: Weak Hand Isolation*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 100 dribbles weak hand only
- Description: Exclusive work with non-dominant hand
- Coaching Cues:
  - Same form as strong hand
  - Various heights (ankle, waist, shoulder)
  - Move in all directions
  - Don't cheat and switch hands
- Progression: Weak-hand only finishing drills

**Level 2 - Intermediate (Weeks 5-8)**

*Drill 1.4: Cone Weave Combinations*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 8 runs through course
- Setup: 5-6 cones in line, 5 feet apart
- Pattern examples:
  - Crossover at each cone
  - Between legs, behind back alternating
  - Double move at each cone
- Coaching Cues:
  - Game speed
  - Head up, cones in peripheral vision
  - Tight to cones (defender simulation)
  - Explode out of moves
- Progression: Tighter spacing, add defender reaction

*Drill 1.5: Live Dribble Moves vs. Defense*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 moves (10 each side)
- Description: 1v1 with defender, create separation with moves
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read defender's positioning
  - Attack their front foot
  - Use move to create driving lane
  - Finish at rim or pull-up
- Progression: Increase defensive intensity, add help defender

*Drill 1.6: Change of Pace Mastery*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 12 sequences
- Description: Slow-fast-slow dribbling to manipulate defender
- Coaching Cues:
  - Extreme contrast (slow = very slow, fast = explosive)
  - Use on-off speed as move itself
  - Lull defender then attack
  - Change of direction often follows change of pace
- Progression: Game situations, defenders reacting

**Level 3 - Advanced (Weeks 9-12)**

*Drill 1.7: Reactive Ball-Handling*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 10 rounds of 30 seconds
- Description: Partner calls out moves, execute immediately
- Moves: Any combination, high difficulty
- Coaching Cues:
  - Zero hesitation
  - Process and execute in <0.5 seconds
  - Maintain balance and control
  - Simulate game chaos
- Progression: Add defensive pressure, time constraints

*Drill 1.8: Advanced Combination Chains*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 8 full-court sequences
- Combinations:
  - Hesitation → crossover → behind back → finish
  - In-and-out → between legs → spin → step-back jumper
  - Crossover → between legs (same hand) → euro step
- Coaching Cues:
  - Flow between moves seamlessly
  - Each move sets up the next
  - Read defender, adjust chain
  - Game speed only
- Progression: Live defenders, 5v5 implementation

#### 2. PICK AND ROLL

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 2.1: Stationary PnR Reads*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 30 reads (10 each coverage)
- Coverages: Hedge, drop, switch
- Description: Stand in PnR position, defender simulates coverage, make correct read
- Coaching Cues:
  - Hedge: Pocket pass or floater
  - Drop: Pull-up jumper
  - Switch: Attack mismatch or re-screen
- Decision time: <2 seconds
- Progression: Add movement, live defenders

*Drill 2.2: Two-Pass Decision Tree*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 PnRs
- Options: Pass to roller or keep
- Description: Simple binary decision based on hedge defender
- Coaching Cues:
  - If hedge hard: pass to roller
  - If drop: keep and shoot/attack
  - Read with eyes, not predetermined
- Progression: Add third option (kick-out)

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 2.3: Live PnR with Rotating Defense*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 25 PnRs
- Setup: Ball handler, screener, 2 defenders, 1 help defender
- Description: Execute PnR with live defense and help rotation
- Reads:
  - Primary: Roller if open
  - Secondary: Pull-up if space
  - Tertiary: Kick to help-side if rotation comes
- Coaching Cues:
  - Keep two defenders on you as long as possible
  - Make defense commit before passing
  - Attack downhill
  - Use screener's body
- Progression: Add more help defenders, faster rotations

*Drill 2.4: Snake and Split Options*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 20 total (10 snake, 10 split)
- Description:
  - Snake: Reject screen, use as decoy, go opposite direction
  - Split: Screener slips early, bounce pass for layup
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read screener's defender positioning
  - If showing high: snake
  - If sagging: split
  - Quick decisions
- Progression: Coverage disguises, live 5v5

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 2.5: Coverage-Specific Counters*
- Duration: 25 minutes
- Reps: 30 PnRs (6 each coverage type)
- Coverages: Hedge, drop, switch, blitz, ice
- Description: Mastery of optimal counter to each coverage
- Answers:
  - Hedge: Pocket or re-screen
  - Drop: Pull-up
  - Switch: Mismatch or re-screen
  - Blitz: Hit roller or kick weak-side
  - Ice: Reject and middle or use screen anyway
- Coaching Cues:
  - ID coverage pre-screen if possible
  - Immediate counter, no hesitation
  - Communication with screener
- Progression: Disguised coverages, rapid-fire reps

*Drill 2.6: Weak-Side Manipulation*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 20 PnRs
- Setup: Full 5v5 or 4v4
- Description: Use PnR to create weak-side advantage
- Coaching Cues:
  - Drag help from weak side with penetration
  - Reverse pivot, find weak-side shooter
  - Advanced passes (skip pass, pocket pass)
  - Create 2-on-1 advantages
- Progression: Better defense, smaller windows

#### 3. SHOOTING

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 3.1: Form Shooting Close Range*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 100 makes
- Distance: 5-8 feet from basket
- Description: Perfect form, high makes, build confidence
- Coaching Cues:
  - BEEF: Balance, Eyes, Elbow, Follow-through
  - Consistent release point
  - Arc (45+ degrees)
  - Backspin on ball
- Progression: Increase distance slowly

*Drill 3.2: Catch-and-Shoot Spots*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 10 makes from 5 spots (50 total makes)
- Spots: Both corners, both wings, top of key
- Description: Partner pass, catch in rhythm, shoot immediately
- Coaching Cues:
  - Hands ready before catch
  - Feet set on catch (1-2 step)
  - Same form every time
  - Quick release
- Progression: Add movement before catch, increase range

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 3.3: Off-Dribble Pull-Ups*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 8 makes from 5 spots (40 total makes)
- Description: Dribble into spot, rise into jumper
- Coaching Cues:
  - Gather on two feet
  - Square shoulders to rim
  - Same form as catch-and-shoot
  - Balance critical
- Variations: 1-dribble pull-up, step-back, side-step
- Progression: Increase difficulty of moves, add defense

*Drill 3.4: Game-Spot Shooting Circuit*
- Duration: 25 minutes
- Reps: 200 total makes
- Description: Simulate game shots from game locations at game speed
- Includes:
  - Catch-and-shoot: 75 makes
  - Off-dribble: 50 makes
  - PnR pull-ups: 35 makes
  - Floaters: 20 makes
  - Free throws: 20 makes
- Coaching Cues:
  - Game speed
  - Variety of spots
  - Track percentages
  - Mental focus on every shot
- Progression: Add defenders, fatigue (shoot after conditioning)

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 3.5: Contested Shooting*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 100 total shots (track makes)
- Description: Defender closes out or contests every shot
- Coaching Cues:
  - Create space with footwork
  - High release vs. contest
  - Don't rush because of defender
  - Maintain form under pressure
- Progression: Better defenders, tighter contests

*Drill 3.6: Fatigue Shooting*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Structure: Conditioning drill followed immediately by shooting
- Reps: 5 rounds (sprint/defensive slides → 10 shots)
- Description: Simulate end-of-game fatigue shooting
- Coaching Cues:
  - Control breathing before shooting
  - Mental toughness
  - Legs might be tired, arms aren't
  - Form doesn't change
- Progression: Longer fatigue periods, game situations

#### 4. FINISHING

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 4.1: Basic Layup Form*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 20 makes each side
- Description: Standard layup footwork and form
- Coaching Cues:
  - Right hand = right foot, left hand = left foot
  - Use backboard
  - High release
  - Soft touch
- Progression: Add speed, add one defender

*Drill 4.2: Floater Introduction*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 15 makes
- Distance: 8-12 feet from basket
- Description: One-hand push shot over imaginary big man
- Coaching Cues:
  - Off two feet or one foot (preference)
  - High arc, soft touch
  - Release at peak of jump
  - Wrist snap, not push
- Progression: Add defense, various angles

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 4.3: Finishing Through Contact*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 makes
- Description: Defender applies legal contact, finish anyway
- Coaching Cues:
  - Expect contact, don't avoid it
  - Protect ball with off-hand and body
  - Finish strong through
  - And-one mentality
- Progression: Increase contact level, multiple defenders

*Drill 4.4: Reverse and Acrobatic Finishes*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 10 makes each variation
- Variations: Reverse layup, euro step, hop step, double clutch
- Coaching Cues:
  - Use when primary blocked
  - Change shot angle or timing
  - Creativity with body control
  - Practice makes instinctual
- Progression: Tight defense, combination moves

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 4.5: English and Touch Mastery*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 40 total makes
- Description: Advanced use of backboard angles and soft touch
- Includes:
  - High-glass finishes
  - Low-glass reverse
  - Finger rolls
  - Teardrops
- Coaching Cues:
  - Backboard is friend
  - Softer touch beats shotblocker
  - Know angles from experience
  - Each angle requires different spot
- Progression: Full speed, live defense

## CENTER BACK (SOCCER) - COMPLETE EXERCISE LIBRARY

### TECHNICAL SKILLS PROGRESSION

#### 1. DEFENDING FUNDAMENTALS

**Level 1 - Beginner (Weeks 1-4)**

*Drill 1.1: Defensive Stance and Footwork*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 5 sets of 30-second movements
- Description: Proper body position and foot movement without ball
- Coaching Cues:
  - Low center of gravity, knees bent
  - Weight on balls of feet
  - Arms out for balance and to show size
  - Quick shuffle steps, don't cross feet
  - Face attacker, maintain distance
- Progression: Add attacker movement to mirror

*Drill 1.2: Jockeying (Delay Tactics)*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 10 reps of 30-second jockeys
- Description: Slow attacker without committing to tackle
- Coaching Cues:
  - Stay goal-side of attacker
  - Force away from goal or to weak foot
  - Maintain 1-2 yard distance
  - Patience, don't dive in
  - Guide toward help or sideline
- Setup: Attacker dribbles slowly, defender jockeys backward
- Progression: Increase attacker speed, add ball

*Drill 1.3: 1v1 Tackle Timing*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 tackles
- Description: When and how to commit to tackle
- Coaching Cues:
  - Wait for attacker's heavy touch
  - Approach with timing
  - Block tackle: strong foot through ball
  - Poke tackle: Quick jab with toe
  - Win ball cleanly, avoid fouls
- Progression: Faster attackers, penalty area scenarios

**Level 2 - Intermediate (Weeks 5-8)**

*Drill 1.4: Step-Cover Partnership*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 15 scenarios
- Setup: Two center backs vs. one or two attackers
- Description: Communication and covering partner
- Coaching Cues:
  - Stepping defender: Engage attacker aggressively
  - Covering defender: Position to intercept pass or cover beaten partner
  - Constant communication ("step" / "cover")
  - Angles and distances critical
  - Trust partner
- Progression: 2v2 scenarios, faster play

*Drill 1.5: Recovery Runs*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 10 recovery sprints
- Description: Sprinting back to goal-side position when beaten
- Coaching Cues:
  - Immediate recognition of being beaten
  - Sprint at maximum effort
  - Angle run to intercept at penalty area
  - Don't give up on play
  - Recover to ball-side of attacker
- Setup: Start 10 yards upfield, attacker beats you, recover
- Progression: Multiple attackers, realistic game scenarios

**Level 3 - Advanced (Weeks 9-12)**

*Drill 1.6: 1v1 Mastery - Advanced Attackers*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 15 defensive situations
- Description: Defend against skilled dribblers with moves
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read attacker's hips and shoulders
  - Don't bite on fakes
  - Stay on feet as long as possible
  - Use physicality legally (shoulder-to-shoulder)
  - Force to weaker option
- Progression: Elite attackers, penalty area defending

*Drill 1.7: Defending in Channels*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 12 scenarios
- Description: Use sideline as extra defender, force attacker wide
- Coaching Cues:
  - Body position: show inside, force outside
  - Trap against sideline
  - Prevent cut back to middle
  - Angle approach to limit options
- Setup: Attacker starts near sideline, CB defends
- Progression: Allow attacker more space, 2v1 with overlapping fullback

#### 2. AERIAL PLAY

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 2.1: Heading Technique - Stationary*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 30 headers
- Description: Partner tosses ball, header back
- Coaching Cues:
  - Eyes open, watch ball onto forehead
  - Contact with hairline/forehead, not top of head
  - Neck muscles tight
  - Head through ball, don't let ball hit head
  - Use whole body, not just neck
- Distance: 5-8 yards
- Progression: Increase power, increase distance

*Drill 2.2: Jumping and Timing*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 20 jumps
- Description: Practice jump timing without opposition
- Coaching Cues:
  - Read ball's flight early
  - Time jump to meet ball at highest point
  - Use arms for elevation and momentum
  - Single or double-leg takeoff (situation dependent)
  - Land balanced
- Progression: Add partner to jump with (not against)

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 2.3: Aerial Duels with Opposition*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 duels
- Description: Compete for ball against opponent
- Coaching Cues:
  - Establish position early (goal-side)
  - Use body to shield opponent
  - Attack ball at highest point
  - Strong, powerful header
  - Direction and distance more than accuracy
- Setup: Server delivers crosses or long balls, 1v1 aerial duel
- Progression: Increase crossing speed, add second attacker

*Drill 2.4: Clearance Technique*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 25 clearances
- Description: Head ball high, far, and wide
- Coaching Cues:
  - Get underneath ball for height
  - Head through and up
  - Target: toward sideline and far
  - Distance critical in penalty area
  - "When in doubt, put it out"
- Progression: Pressure from attacker, various service types

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 2.5: Offensive Aerial Play*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 attempts
- Description: Attacking headers on corners/free kicks
- Coaching Cues:
  - Lose marker with movement
  - Attack ball aggressively
  - Head down and on target
  - Use upper body strength to create space
  - Timing run to arrive at peak
- Setup: Corners and free kicks with goalkeeper
- Progression: Live defenders, competitive scenarios

*Drill 2.6: Aerial Duels Under Pressure*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 20 duels
- Description: Win headers while being physically challenged
- Coaching Cues:
  - Anticipate contact
  - Hold ground and position
  - Use legal physicality (shoulder, not hands)
  - Stay focused on ball, not opponent
  - Win first ball or position for second ball
- Setup: Realistic game pressure, multiple attackers
- Progression: High-level attackers, game scenarios

#### 3. PASSING AND DISTRIBUTION

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 3.1: Short Passing Accuracy*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Reps: 50 passes (25 each foot)
- Distance: 10-15 yards
- Description: Simple passing back and forth with partner
- Coaching Cues:
  - Inside of foot, firm pass
  - Knee over ball
  - Follow through to target
  - Weight of pass: firm but receivable
- Progression: Increase distance, add movement

*Drill 3.2: Receiving Under Pressure Basics*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 30 receives and passes
- Description: Receive ball with light pressure, pass to target
- Coaching Cues:
  - Check shoulder before receiving
  - Body shape: open to field
  - First touch away from pressure
  - Quick second touch (pass)
- Setup: Passer, receiver, light pressure defender
- Progression: Increase defensive pressure, vary passing angles

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 3.3: Long Diagonal Passing*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 30 passes
- Distance: 30-40 yards diagonally
- Description: Switch point of attack with accurate long balls
- Coaching Cues:
  - Approach angle (not straight on)
  - Strike with laces, leg through ball
  - Lean back slightly for elevation
  - Aim for chest/feet of target
  - Weight: must travel but be controlled
- Setup: Half-field, targets at opposite sides
- Progression: Longer distance, moving targets, pressure

*Drill 3.4: Driven Passes into Midfield*
- Duration: 12 minutes
- Reps: 25 passes
- Distance: 20-30 yards into midfielder feet
- Description: Build from back with progressive passes
- Coaching Cues:
  - Driven ball (low, fast)
  - Accuracy to feet
  - Play into space if midfielder checking
  - Read pressure on midfielder
  - Alternative: pass to fullback if blocked
- Progression: Add pressure, live midfielders checking

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 3.5: Distribution Under High Press*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 30 possessions
- Description: Receive from GK, evade press, progress ball
- Coaching Cues:
  - Constant scanning
  - Body position to see field
  - Quick decision (1-2 touch)
  - Options: Pass back, wide, split press, or dribble
  - Composure under pressure
- Setup: GK + 2 CBs vs. 2-3 pressing attackers
- Progression: Increase pressing intensity, full 11v11 buildup

*Drill 3.6: Breaking Lines with Passes*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 passes
- Description: Play passes that break opponent's defensive lines
- Coaching Cues:
  - Identify space between lines
  - Weight and timing critical
  - Driven passes often needed
  - Communication with receiver
  - Risk/reward assessment
- Progression: Tighter defensive lines, game scenarios

#### 4. POSITIONING AND TACTICAL AWARENESS

**Level 1 - Beginner**

*Drill 4.1: Zonal Positioning Basics*
- Duration: 15 minutes (walk-through)
- Description: Understand center back positioning relative to ball
- Coaching Cues:
  - Always between ball and goal
  - Adjust position as ball moves
  - Depth and width based on ball location
  - Communication with partner
- Setup: Walking through various ball positions on field
- Progression: Jogging tempo, add attackers

*Drill 4.2: Communication Practice*
- Duration: 10 minutes
- Description: Verbal communication with backline
- Commands to practice:
  - "Away" - clear the ball
  - "Step" or "Cover" - organize with partner
  - "Man on" - alert teammate to pressure
  - "Turn" - space available to turn
  - "Line" - organize offside line
- Progression: Noisy environment, game situations

**Level 2 - Intermediate**

*Drill 4.3: Offside Line Management*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 scenarios
- Description: Organize and maintain defensive line
- Coaching Cues:
  - Talk constantly ("hold," "push," "drop")
  - Everyone steps together
  - Watch ball and runners
  - Deliberate: Drop deep or hold high based on situation
  - Awareness of teammates' positions
- Setup: 4 defenders vs attackers, focus on line
- Progression: Faster attackers, through-ball attempts

*Drill 4.4: Triggered Pressing*
- Duration: 18 minutes
- Reps: 15 pressing scenarios
- Description: When to step and press high as unit
- Triggers:
  - Poor touch by opponent
  - Predictable pass
  - Opponent facing own goal
  - Numerical advantage
- Coaching Cues:
  - Entire line steps together
  - Close space quickly
  - Force mistake or win ball
  - If broken, immediate recovery
- Progression: Better opponents, realistic scenarios

**Level 3 - Advanced**

*Drill 4.5: Rest-Defense Organization*
- Duration: 20 minutes
- Reps: 15 transition scenarios
- Description: Organizing defense during opponent possession
- Coaching Cues:
  - Immediate shape when possession lost
  - Distances: compact, 10-15 yard gaps
  - Depth: Based on speed of attackers
  - Communication: Constant
  - Prevent penetration centrally
- Setup: Full team or 8v8, focus on defensive shape
- Progression: Quick transitions, elite attackers

*Drill 4.6: Covering Space vs. Covering Player*
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reps: 20 decisions
- Description: When to mark tightly vs. hold position
- Decision matrix:
  - Ball close + attacker in space = tight marking
  - Ball far + attacker deep = zonal covering
  - Through-ball threat = split difference
- Coaching Cues:
  - Anticipate opponent's intent
  - Balance risk/reward
  - Trust teammates to cover
  - Adjust with game state (winning vs. losing)
- Progression: Complex scenarios, game situations

## STRENGTH & CONDITIONING EXERCISES

### QUARTERBACK-SPECIFIC STRENGTH EXERCISES

*Exercise 1: Rear-Foot Elevated Split Squat*
- Primary muscle groups: Quads, glutes, hip stabilizers
- Purpose: Drop-back power, single-leg strength for throwing base
- Setup: Back foot on bench (12-18"), front foot flat
- Execution:
  - Lower until front thigh parallel
  - Drive through front heel to stand
  - Maintain upright torso
  - Load: Dumbbells in hands or barbell on back
- Sets/Reps: 3-4 sets × 6-8 reps each leg
- Progression: Increase weight, add pause at bottom, explosive concentric

*Exercise 2: Anti-Rotation Pallof Press*
- Primary: Core stability, anti-rotation strength
- Purpose: Maintain stable base during rotational throwing motion
- Setup: Cable or band at chest height, stand perpendicular
- Execution:
  - Hold handle at chest
  - Press straight out, resist rotation
  - Hold 2 seconds, return to chest
  - Don't let torso twist
- Sets/Reps: 3 sets × 10-12 reps each side
- Progression: Increase resistance, single-leg stance, add movement

*Exercise 3: Medicine Ball Rotational Throw*
- Primary: Rotational power, hip-to-shoulder force transfer
- Purpose: Explosive throwing power
- Setup: Stand sideways to wall, med ball at hip
- Execution:
  - Explosive hip rotation
  - Transfer through core
  - Release ball into wall
  - Catch and repeat
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 6-8 reps each side
- Weight: 8-12 lbs for power (not max strength)
- Progression: Heavier ball, increase distance, single-leg stance

### POINT GUARD-SPECIFIC STRENGTH EXERCISES

*Exercise 1: Pause Back Squat*
- Primary: Quad strength, deceleration capacity
- Purpose: Absorb landing forces, change direction ability
- Setup: Barbell on back, feet shoulder-width
- Execution:
  - Descend slowly (3 seconds)
  - Pause at bottom (3 seconds)
  - Explosive stand
  - Full depth (hip crease below knee)
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 6-8 reps
- Progression: Increase weight, longer pause, add jump at top

*Exercise 2: Nordic Hamstring Curl*
- Primary: Hamstring eccentric strength
- Purpose: Deceleration, knee health, injury prevention
- Setup: Knees on pad, partner holds ankles
- Execution:
  - Slowly lower torso forward
  - Resist with hamstrings as long as possible
  - Catch with hands, push back up
  - Focus on eccentric (lowering) phase
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 4-6 reps
- Progression: Slower tempo, banded assistance, less assist from hands

*Exercise 3: Trap Bar Jump*
- Primary: Explosive lower body power
- Purpose: Vertical jump, first-step quickness
- Setup: Trap bar loaded, stand inside
- Execution:
  - Quarter squat dip
  - Explosive jump upward
  - Land softly, reset
  - Load: 30-50% of deadlift max
- Sets/Reps: 4-5 sets × 3-4 reps
- Progression: Increase weight (but maintain velocity), single-leg variation

### CENTER BACK-SPECIFIC STRENGTH EXERCISES

*Exercise 1: Front Squat*
- Primary: Quad and core strength
- Purpose: Jumping power, overall lower body strength
- Setup: Barbell on front of shoulders, elbows high
- Execution:
  - Descend with upright torso
  - Full depth
  - Drive through heels to stand
  - Elbows stay high throughout
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 6-8 reps
- Progression: Increase weight, add pause, tempo variations

*Exercise 2: Copenhagen Plank*
- Primary: Adductor strength
- Purpose: Groin injury prevention, lateral movement strength
- Setup: Side plank with top leg on bench
- Execution:
  - Lower leg lifted off ground (or on ground for easier)
  - Hold position using adductors
  - Maintain straight body line
  - Don't let hip sag
- Sets/Reps: 3 sets × 20-30 seconds each side
- Progression: Longer holds, add top-leg lifts, weighted

*Exercise 3: Loaded Box Jump*
- Primary: Explosive lower body, aerial power
- Purpose: Heading ability, jumping in traffic
- Setup: Weighted vest or light dumbbells, box 18-24"
- Execution:
  - Arm swing and jump onto box
  - Full hip extension at top
  - Step down (don't jump down)
  - Load: 10-20 lbs
- Sets/Reps: 4 sets × 3-5 reps
- Progression: Higher box, more weight, single-leg variation

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

**Video Content Requirements**:
- Each drill should have 60-90 second demonstration video
- Multiple camera angles for complex drills
- Slow-motion replays of key coaching points
- Common errors shown and corrected
- Progressive difficulty shown in same video

**Exercise Substitutions**:
- Every exercise should have 2-3 alternatives for:
  - Equipment limitations (home gym vs. full facility)
  - Injury modifications
  - Experience level adjustments

**Progress Tracking**:
- Skills: Completion percentage, success rate, time to complete
- Strength: Weight, reps, RPE, video analysis
- Metrics: Regular testing (weekly skills, monthly strength)

**Integration with Main Framework**:
- These exercises populate the weekly plans in main framework
- App selects exercises based on:
  - Athlete's experience level
  - Phase of training (accumulation/intensification/realization)
  - Available equipment
  - Identified weaknesses

This library provides the detailed, actionable content that makes the training curriculum framework operational at scale.
