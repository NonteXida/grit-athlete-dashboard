# Getting Started with AthleteHub

## Quick Start Guide

### 1. Create Your Account

1. Open the application
2. Click "Sign up" on the authentication page
3. Select your user type:
   - **Athlete**: Full access to training, nutrition, media, and goals
   - **Coach**: View athlete profiles and provide feedback
   - **Parent**: Limited access to your child's profile

4. Fill in your information:
   - Full Name
   - Email Address
   - Password
   - Sport (for athletes)
   - Graduation Year (for athletes)
   - School Name (for athletes)

5. Click "Create Account"

### 2. Complete Your Profile (Athletes)

Once signed in, navigate to your Profile page:

1. **Upload Photos**
   - Click the camera icon on your cover photo to upload a hero image
   - Click the camera icon on your profile picture to add a headshot

2. **Write Your Bio**
   - Click "Edit Profile"
   - Share your athletic journey, goals, and what drives you
   - Highlight your character and values, not just stats

3. **Add Your Stats**
   - Height, Weight, Position, Jersey Number
   - GPA and academic achievements
   - These help recruiters understand your full profile

4. **Character & Values**
   - Showcase traits like Leadership, Work Ethic, Coachability
   - Provide specific examples of each trait
   - This differentiates you from other athletes

5. **Achievement Timeline**
   - Add your athletic achievements (All-State, MVP, Championships)
   - Include character achievements (Team Captain, Community Service)
   - Provide context for each achievement

6. **Upload Highlight Reel**
   - Add videos of your best game moments
   - Keep videos under 5 minutes for better engagement
   - Add descriptive titles and tags

### 3. Track Your Training

Navigate to the Training Hub:

1. **View Your Weekly Schedule**
   - See your planned workouts for the week
   - Click on any day to see the full workout details

2. **Log Workouts**
   - Click "Log Workout" to record a completed session
   - Enter exercises, sets, reps, and weight
   - Add notes about how you felt or any challenges

3. **Track Progress**
   - View progress charts for key lifts
   - Monitor improvement over time
   - Celebrate milestones and PRs (Personal Records)

4. **Browse Exercise Library**
   - Find new exercises for your sport
   - Filter by category (Upper Body, Lower Body, etc.)
   - Learn proper form and technique

### 4. Monitor Nutrition

Navigate to the Nutrition Module:

1. **Log Your Meals**
   - Click "Log Meal" after eating
   - Add food items with nutritional information
   - Track breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks

2. **Track Macros**
   - Monitor calories, protein, carbs, and fats
   - View progress rings showing daily goals
   - Adjust targets based on your training phase

3. **Stay Hydrated**
   - Log water intake throughout the day
   - Aim for 8-10 glasses (64-80 oz) daily
   - More on training days

4. **Manage Supplements**
   - Track your supplement schedule
   - Check off when taken
   - Include protein, creatine, vitamins, etc.

### 5. Set and Achieve Goals

Navigate to Goal Tracking:

1. **Create SMART Goals**
   - Specific: "Bench Press 225 lbs" not "Get stronger"
   - Measurable: Include numbers you can track
   - Achievable: Challenging but realistic
   - Relevant: Aligned with your sport and position
   - Time-bound: Set a deadline

2. **Track Progress**
   - Update your current progress regularly
   - Watch your progress percentage increase
   - Celebrate milestone achievements

3. **Weekly Check-ins**
   - Share how you're feeling about your goals
   - Describe challenges you faced
   - Recognize your wins, no matter how small

4. **Coach Feedback**
   - Read feedback from your coaches
   - Ask questions or request advice
   - Implement suggested adjustments

### 6. Build Your Media Library

Navigate to Media Center:

1. **Upload Highlights**
   - Click "Upload Media"
   - Select video or photo
   - Add title and description
   - Choose visibility (public/private/team-only)

2. **Organize Content**
   - Tag videos by event, opponent, or skill
   - Create collections for different seasons
   - Keep your best content easily accessible

3. **Share with Recruiters**
   - Use the share button to send links
   - Track views and engagement
   - Update regularly with new content

4. **Analytics**
   - Monitor how many views your highlights get
   - See which content performs best
   - Optimize based on what coaches engage with

### 7. Understanding Your Grit Score

Your Grit Score measures dedication and growth mindset, not just athletic performance.

**Components:**
- **Consistency (30%)**: Show up and complete workouts regularly
- **Improvement (30%)**: Progress toward your goals
- **Effort (25%)**: Push yourself in challenging workouts
- **Accountability (15%)**: Honest check-ins and self-assessment

**How to Improve:**
- Complete scheduled workouts consistently
- Log all training sessions honestly
- Make progress toward your goals
- Complete weekly check-ins
- Push yourself during training

**Why It Matters:**
College coaches value character and work ethic as much as athletic ability. Your Grit Score demonstrates your commitment to excellence.

## Tips for Success

### For Athletes

1. **Be Consistent**
   - Log into the platform daily
   - Track all workouts and meals
   - Update progress regularly

2. **Be Honest**
   - Record actual weights and reps
   - Share real challenges in check-ins
   - Authenticity builds trust with coaches

3. **Tell Your Story**
   - Your profile is more than stats
   - Share what makes you unique
   - Highlight character alongside achievements

4. **Engage with Coaches**
   - Respond to feedback
   - Ask questions when needed
   - Show appreciation for guidance

5. **Set the Right Goals**
   - Balance athletic and academic goals
   - Include character development goals
   - Make them challenging but achievable

### For Coaches

1. **Provide Regular Feedback**
   - Comment on athlete progress
   - Offer specific suggestions
   - Celebrate improvements and effort

2. **Assign Appropriate Programs**
   - Match workouts to athlete's level
   - Progress difficulty over time
   - Consider sport-specific needs

3. **Monitor the Grit Score**
   - Recognize consistent athletes
   - Encourage those struggling
   - Use it as a teaching opportunity

### For Parents

1. **Support Without Pressure**
   - Celebrate effort, not just results
   - Encourage consistency
   - Respect their privacy settings

2. **Monitor Without Micromanaging**
   - Check in on progress periodically
   - Let athletes own their journey
   - Provide encouragement and support

3. **Understand the Full Picture**
   - Athletic development takes time
   - Character matters as much as performance
   - The process is as important as results

## Common Questions

**Q: How often should I log workouts?**
A: Log every workout, ideally right after completing it while details are fresh.

**Q: What should I include in my bio?**
A: Your athletic journey, what drives you, your goals, and what makes you unique. Include character traits and values.

**Q: How do I increase my Grit Score?**
A: Be consistent with workouts, make progress on goals, complete check-ins honestly, and push yourself during training.

**Q: Can I make my profile private?**
A: Yes, you can control visibility for your entire profile and individual media items.

**Q: How do I share my profile with college recruiters?**
A: Set your profile to public and share the link. Include your highlight reel and ensure all information is current.

**Q: What if I miss a workout?**
A: That's okay! What matters is getting back on track. Log the missed workout and continue with your schedule.

**Q: How detailed should my nutrition logging be?**
A: As detailed as helpful. At minimum, track total calories and protein. More detail helps with optimization.

**Q: Can coaches see everything on my profile?**
A: Only coaches you've assigned can see your profile. You control who has access.
